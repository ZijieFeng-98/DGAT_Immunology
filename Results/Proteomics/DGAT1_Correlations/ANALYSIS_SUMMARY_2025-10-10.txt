FINDING 2: SIGNIFICANT LIPID METABOLISM CORRELATIONS ✅
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. DGAT1 ↔ SREBF1 (SREBP-1): r = +0.336, p_adj = 0.020 ✅
   → Positive correlation with lipogenesis master regulator
   → Coordinated triglyceride and fatty acid synthesis
   → Supports lipid droplet formation phenotype

2. DGAT1 ↔ SREBF2 (SREBP-2): r = -0.354, p_adj = 0.016 ✅
   → Negative correlation with cholesterol synthesis
   → Metabolic trade-off: TAG synthesis ↑ Cholesterol ↓
   → GBM cells favor TAG storage when DGAT1 high

BIOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PROTEIN-LEVEL DISCONNECT FROM IMMUNE SYSTEM
   • DGAT1 protein does not correlate with immune proteins
   • Contrasts with potential mRNA-level relationships
   • May reflect:
     - Post-transcriptional regulation
     - Protein stability differences
     - Compartmentalization effects
     - Limited detection of immune cell proteins

2. DGAT1 IS PART OF LIPID SYNTHESIS PROGRAM
   • Strong coordination with SREBP-1 (lipogenesis)
   • Inverse relationship with SREBP-2 (cholesterol)
   • Confirms role in triglyceride metabolism pathway

3. METABOLIC REPROGRAMMING IN GBM
   • High DGAT1 tumors favor TAG synthesis over cholesterol
   • Supports lipid droplet accumulation phenotype
   • Consistent with Dr. Guo's lab PLIN/lipid droplet focus
